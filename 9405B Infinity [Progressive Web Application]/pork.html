Pork Adobo

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

2 lbs. pork cut into bite-size pieces
2 tbsp vegetable oil
3 cloves garlic, minced
1/3 cup soy sauce
1/3 cup vinegar
1 bay leaf
1/2 tsp ground pepper
water
1 tbsp sugar or honey

Instructions

Heat oil in a large pot, rotate to coat sides of the pot, Add pork and cook until the meat is brown

Add garlic and saute for a minute. Add soy sauce, vinegar, bay leaf, pepper and water

Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer for 45 minutes or until pork are tender(dont let it dry out) add water 1/2 cup at a time several times until pork meat is tender. Stir in honey or sugar.

Serve with steamed rice

Binagoongan

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

1 1/2 pork belly cut into cubes
2 pcs. tomato cubed
1 pc. Chinese Eggplant
3 1/2 cups pork stock
3 tbsps. white vinegar
4 tbs bagoong alamang
1 pc onion chopped
3 cloves garlic minced
2 tsps. white sugar
1/8 tsp. groung black pepper
5 tbsps. cooking oil

Instructions

Heat oil in cooking pan

Saute onion, garlic, and tomato. Once the onion becomes soft, add the pork belly. Cook until the belly turns light brown

Add shrimp paste(bagoong alamang). Stir. Cook for 5 mins.

Pour vinegar.Let the mixture boil. stir and cook for 2 mins.

Pour the pork stock into the pot. Cover and let boil. Adjust the heat to medium and continue to cook for 40 mins. until the pork gets tenders. Note: add water or pork stock as needed.

Add eggplant. Stir.Cover the pot and cook for 3 mins.

Season with sugar and ground black pepper.

transfer to a plate and serve.

Pork Dumpling

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

1 lb. ground pork
1 1/2 tbsps. knorr liquid seasoning
30 pcs Gyoza rapper
1 cup cabbage chopped
1/2 cup shrimp chopped
1/2 cup Chinese chives chopped
2 tbsps. cooking wine
1 tbsp. ginger grated
1 tsp. sesame oil
1/2 tsp. salt
1/4 tsp. groung white peppers
6 cups of water

Instructions

Mash the cabbage. Add salt. Continue to mash until salt is well distributed. Let it stay for 10 minutes.

Combine ground pork, shrimp, ginger, and chives in bowl. Mix well.

Add cabbage, ground white pepper, cooking wine, Sesame oil, and Knorr liquid seasoning. Continue to mix until all ingredients are well blended.

Scoop 1 ½ teaspoon of pork mixture and place it in the middle of the Gyoza wrapper. Secure the filling by symmetrically folding the wrapper and pressing the edges. You may also press using the tip of a fork.

Arrange the pork dumplings on a steamer lined with cabbage leaves. Cover and steam for 10 minutes

Serve with dipping sauce

Pata Hamonado

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

3 1/2 ls pork hock pata, sliced
1 piece Knorr Pork Cube
20 ounces pineapple chunks
1 piece onion
4 cloves garlic
1/4 cup soy sauce
1/4 cup brown sugar
5 pieces bay leaves
1 1/2 cups water
1 tsp. whole peppercorn
3 tbsps. cooking oil
salt to taste

Instructions

Boil 4 cups of water in a cooking pot. Add pork hock. Let boil for 15 minutes. Discard water. Set the pork aside

Heat cooking oil in a cooking pot. Saute garlic and onion.

Add the boiled pork hock once the onion softens.

Pour soy sauce, pineapple juice, and water. Let boil. Add whole peppercorn and dried bay leaves Pressure cook for 18 minutes. Note: if not using a pressure cooker, boil pork for 90 minutes on a regular cooking pot. Make sure to add water if the liquid is about to dry-up.

Add Knorr Pork Cube, brown sugar, and pineapple chunks. Stir. Let it simmer until the sauce reduces to half.

Season with salt. Transfer to a serving plate. Serve with rice.

Lechon Kawali

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

4 lbs. pork belly
2 tablespoon salt
1 head garlic, pounded
2 tablespoon peppercorn
2 bay leaves
cooking oil
water

Instructions

In a pot over medium heat, combine pork belly, garlic, salt, peppercorns, bay leaves, and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil, skimming scum that floats on top. Lower heat, cover and continue to cook for about 1 to 1-1/2 hours or until meat is tender but not falling apart.

Drain pork from liquid, discarding liquid. Wipe the pork dry, removing any stray aromatics. Place on a rack and refrigerate overnight to completely cool and dry.

In a deep, heavy-bottomed pot, heat enough oil to completely cover meat to about 350 F to 375 F. Carefully place meat into oil and fry until meat is golden and skin is crisp and puffed. While the meat is frying, regularly sprinkle about 1 tablespoon cold water onto the meat and hot oil every 5 minutes or so.

Remove meat from pot and drain on a wire rack setover a baking sheet. let it rest and cut into serving pieces.

Crispy Pata

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

1 piece whole pig leg
6 pieces bay leaves
2 tbsp. peppercorn
6 tsps. salt
2 tsps. ground pepper
2 tsps. garlic powder
12 cups of water
6 cups of cooking oil

Instructions

Pour water in a cooking pot then let it boil.

Put-in dried bay leaves, whole peppercorn, and 4 teaspoons of salt.

Add the whole pig’s legs in the cooking pot then simmer until the leg becomes tender (about 45 to 60 minutes)

Remove the tender leg from the cooking pot and set aside until the temperature goes down.

Rub the leg with garlic powder, ground black pepper, and remaining salt. Let stand for 15 minutes to absorb the rub.

Heat a clean large cooking pot (preferably with cover) and pour-in cooking oil.

When the oil becomes hot, deep fry the rubbed pork leg. Continue cooking in medium heat until one side becomes crispy, and then cautiously flip the leg to crisp the other side. Note: Be extra careful in doing this procedure.

Turn-off the heat; remove the crispy pork leg; and transfer it to a wide serving plate.

Serve with atcharang papaya and soy sauce – vinegar dipping sauce.

Pinoy Pork Barbeque

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

6 lbs pork, sliced into 1inch wide and 1/4inch thick
2 liters7-up
soy sauce
vinegar
2 tablespoon ground pepper
6 cups brown sugar
chili peppers
oyster sauce
2 cups banana ketchup
sesame oil
For the spiced vinegar dip
1 cup white vinegar
3 cloves garlic, minced
1/2 onion, chopped
1/8 ground pepper
1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

Rinse pork strips and drain well. Pat dry.

In a large bowl, combine 7-up, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, black pepper, garlic, chili peppers and 1 cup of the oyster sauce.

Add pork and massage meat to fully incorporate. Marinate, turning meat once or twice, in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or overnight for best results

In a bowl, combine remaining 1 cup of oyster sauce, banana ketchup, and sesame oil. Set aside.

Thread 2 to 3 meat slices onto each skewer. Grill meat over hot coals for about 2 to 3 minutes each side.

When pork starts to lose its pink, baste with oyster sauce-ketchup mixture. Continue to grill and baste, turning on sides, until meat is cooked through. Remove from heat and serve as is or with spicy vinegar dip.

For the vinegar dip, combine vinegar, garlic, onion, pepper and salt on a bowl

Sinigang na Baboy with Gabi

Lets Start! Enjoy Cooking

Ingredients

2 lbs. pork
kangkong leaves
green chili pepper
2 pieces tomato
1 cup sliced radish
10 to 12 pieces beans
onion
15 pieces okra
2 pcs. taro
8 cups water
Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc Mix Original
3 tbsp. fish sauce
1/4 tsp. ground pepper

Instructions

Pour water into a cooking pot. Let it boil

Add onion and tomato. Boil for 5 to 8 minutes

Put the pork in the cooking pot.Boil for 25 minutes

Add the taro. Cover and continue to boil for 30 minutes.

Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.

Put the radish in. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.

Add the chili peppers, okra, and snake beans. Cook for 7 minutes.

Add the kangkong. Cover the cooking pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.

Transfer to as serving bowl. Serve with a small bowl with patis and spicy chili.
