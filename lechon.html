Lechon Kawali

Ingredients

4 lbs. pork belly

2 tablespoon salt

1 head garlic, pounded

2 tablespoon peppercorn

2 bay leaves

cooking oil

water

Instructions

1. In a pot over medium heat, combine pork belly, garlic, salt, peppercorns, bay leaves, and enough water to cover. Bring to a boil,

skimming scum that floats on top. Lower heat, cover and continue to cook for about 1 to 1-1/2 hours or until meat is tender but

not falling apart.

2. Drain pork from liquid, discarding liquid. Wipe the pork dry, removing any stray aromatics. Place on a rack and refrigerate

overnight to completely cool and dry

3. In a deep, heavy-bottomed pot, heat enough oil to completely cover meat to about 350 F to 375 F. Carefully place meat into oil

and fry until meat is golden and skin is crisp and puffed. While the meat is frying, regularly sprinkle about 1 tablespoon cold water

onto the meat and hot oil every 5 minutes or so.

4. Remove meat from pot and drain on a wire rack setover a baking sheet. let it rest and cut into serving pieces.

Pinoy Pork Barbeque

Ingredients

6 lbs pork, sliced into 1inch wide and 1/4inch thick

2 liters7-up

soy sauce

vinegar

2 tablespoon ground pepper

6 cups brown sugar

chili peppers

oyster sauce

2 cups banana ketchup

sesame oil

For the spiced vinegar dip

1 cup white vinegar

3 cloves garlic, minced

1/2 onion, chopped

1/8 ground pepper

1/4 tsp salt

Instructions

1. Rinse pork strips and drain well. Pat dry.

2. In a large bowl, combine 7-up, soy sauce, vinegar, brown sugar, black pepper, garlic, chili peppers and 1 cup of the oyster sauce.

3. Add pork and massage meat to fully incorporate. Marinate, turning meat once or twice, in the refrigerator for at least 4 hours or

overnight for best results

4. In a bowl, combine remaining 1 cup of oyster sauce, banana ketchup, and sesame oil. Set aside.

5. Thread 2 to 3 meat slices onto each skewer. Grill meat over hot coals for about 2 to 3 minutes each side.

6. When pork starts to lose its pink, baste with oyster sauce-ketchup mixture. Continue to grill and baste, turning on sides, until meat

is cooked through. Remove from heat and serve as is or with spicy vinegar dip.

7. For the vinegar dip, combine vinegar, garlic, onion, pepper and salt on a bowl

Pork Adobo

Ingredients

2 lbs. pork cut into bite-size pieces

2 tbsp vegetable oil

3 cloves garlic, minced

1/3 cup soy sauce

1/3 cup vinegar

1 bay leaf

1/2 tsp ground pepper

water

1 tbsp sugar or honey

Instructions

1. Heat oil in a large pot, rotate to coat sides of the pot, Add pork and cook until the meat is brown

2. Add garlic and saute for a minute. Add soy sauce, vinegar, bay leaf, pepper and water

3. Bring to a boil, reduce heat and simmer for 45 minutes or until pork are tender(dont let it dry out) add water 1/2 cup at a time several times until pork meat is tender. Stir in honey or sugar

4. Serve with steamed rice
